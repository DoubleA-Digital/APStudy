🎓 Test Day Logistics

Everything you need on exam day.

📆 Exam dates
AP Calculus AB May 8, 2026 · 8:00 AM
AP Computer Science A May 8, 2026 · 12:00 PM
⚠️ Same day, two exams. Lunch break between. Rest well the night before, bring snacks.
⏱️ Exam structure

Calc AB — 3 hr 15 min total

  • MCQ Part A — 30 Q, 60 min, no calculator
  • MCQ Part B — 15 Q, 45 min, calculator allowed
  • FRQ Part A — 2 Q, 30 min, calculator
  • FRQ Part B — 4 Q, 60 min, no calculator

CS A — 3 hr total

  • MCQ — 40 Q, 90 min, no aids (Java quick ref provided)
  • FRQ — 4 Q, 90 min, Java quick ref provided
🎒 What to bring
  • ✓ 2+ sharpened #2 pencils with erasers
  • ✓ 2 black or dark blue ink pens (for FRQs)
  • ✓ Government or school ID
  • ✓ Approved graphing calculator (TI-84 Plus CE recommended)
  • ✓ Spare calculator batteries
  • ✓ Watch (analog only — no smartwatches)
  • ✓ Water bottle (clear)
  • ✓ Light snack for break between exams
🚫 Do NOT bring
  • ✗ Phone, smartwatch, any wireless device
  • ✗ Mechanical pencils (non-approved)
  • ✗ Scratch paper (provided)
  • ✗ Notes, books, or study guides
  • ✗ Food or drink in exam room (except permitted break snacks)
  • ✗ Hats or hoods (unless religious)
💡 Night before & morning of

Night before

  • Pack your bag the night before
  • Review reference sheets — don't cram new material
  • Charge calculator fully
  • Go to bed by 10 PM
  • Set 2 alarms

Morning of

  • Eat a balanced breakfast (protein + carbs)
  • Arrive 30 min early
  • Hit the bathroom before check-in
  • Take 10 deep breaths before starting
  • Skim the easy MCQs first, circle back to tough ones
📝 Scoring

Both exams score 1–5. Most colleges accept 4 or 5 for credit. A 3 is "qualified" and accepted by many.

  • Calc AB: ~60–70% raw → 5, ~45–55% raw → 4, ~35–45% raw → 3
  • CS A: ~65–75% raw → 5, ~50–65% raw → 4, ~35–50% raw → 3
